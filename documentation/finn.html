Persona:

Finn Mertens

Profession:

22y.o college student that works for Uber Eats and Grubhub in his spare time for the past 2 years. Frequently uses mapping applications when navigating to different food pickup and drop-off locations.

Technology:

Cellphone: Surface Duo 2 - Qualcomm Snapdragon 888 5G Mobile Platform, OS Android 11, 256GB SSD

  • Browser(s): Google

Home Computer: Dell Alienware M17 - Intel Core i7-10750H CPU @ 2.6GHz, Windows 11 Home, 1TB SSD

  • Browser(s): Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox

Attitudes and Behaviors:

Ambitious college student that has worked diligently and earned mostly everything he has by his own volition. When not in class or cramming to finish an assignment, is known to undertake various jobs. Loves to earn money. Is currently into saving, investing in cryptocurrencies, and minting NFTs. Currently, does work on food delivery applications. Speeds often and has sometimes had to replace tires or get body work due to damages.

Frustrations and Needs:

Finn loves investing his money and hates when having to make large or unexpected payments. Twice this school year, he has had to take his car in for repairs due to damages from potholes. Finn needs a system for identifying and helping to avoid potholes, being able to mark potholes for future reference, and reporting potholes in hopes that they are filled.

Goals:

Finn is in his vehicle often and has had his fair-share of run-ins with potholes. Finn wants to be able to identify potholes ahead of time, so he can either avoid the route, or be weary and navigate the route cautiously. Finn also wants to be able to mark potholes for other motorists to help them avoid damages. Lastly, Finn heard that the city monitors this application and uses it to set priorities for filling potholes. Finn wants to use the application to post pictures of extremely severe potholes, and get them filled.

User Story:

As a power user, I need to be able to mark and identify potholes on my routes so that I can avoid damaging my car.

Use Case:

Pre-Condition: Finn is on Pothole Finder web-based application.

Post Condition: Finn marks a severe pothole on the application.

Interaction Flow:

  1. Finn clicks "Add Pothole" button.
  2. Website prompts user to drag and drop the pin to the location of the pothole.
  3. Finn drops a pin on the map of the pothole's location.
  4. Website navigates to the add pothole form page.
  5. Finn clicks the "Severe 3+ button".
  6. Website highlights the "Severe 3+" option.
  7. Finn clicks the "Upload a photo" button.
  8. Website asks if user will be uploading via camera or photo album.
  9. Finn clicks upload via camera.
  10. Website opens the camera application.
  11. Finn takes a picture of the pothole and clicks the "Confirm" button.
  12. Website uploads photo to the "Add Pothole" form.
  13. Finn clicks "Done" button.
  14. Website displays a severity symbol on the uploaded pothole location.
